Eight Great Cuisines of China
Cuisine Chinese Name Features
Cantonese Cuisine 粤菜 Yuècài Sweeter, favoring braising and stewing, adding various mild sauces.
Sichuan Cuisine 川菜 Chuāncài Spicy and bold, often mouth-numbing, using lots of chili, garlic, ginger, and peanuts.
Zhejiang Cuisine 浙菜 Zhècài Mellow, using fresh seafood, freshwater fish, and bamboo shoots, and a wide variety of cooking methods.
Jiangsu Cuisine 苏菜 Sūcài Fresh, moderately salty and sweet, precise cooking techniques, favoring seafood, soups and artistic, colorful presentation
Fujian/Min Cuisine 闽菜 Mǐncài Lighter, with a mild sweet and sour taste, using ingredients from the sea and the mountains.
Hunan Cuisine 湘菜 Xiāngcài Uses many wild plants and animals as ingredients, favoring stewing and more oil.
Anhui Cuisine 徽菜 Huīcài Sweeter, favoring braising and stewing, adding various mild sauces.
Shandong Cuisine 鲁菜 Lǔcài Salty and crispy, favoring braising and seafood.
